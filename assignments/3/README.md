## 2.5 Analysing Hyperparameters Effects (15 Marks)

### Effect of Non-Linearity

[Wandb Sweep](https://wandb.ai/vemulasakethreddy_10/MLP_Classification/sweeps/s30e882g/workspace)

Analysis:

1. Performance Hierarchy:
   - Sigmoid > Tanh > ReLU/LeakyReLU
   - Possible explanation: The dataset may contain features or patterns that are better captured by bounded activation functions (sigmoid and tanh) rather than unbounded ones (ReLU and LeakyReLU).

2. Sigmoid's Effectiveness:
   - Highest accuracy and F1 score
   - Possible explanation: The classification problem might involve subtle distinctions near the extremes of the feature space, where sigmoid's S-shaped curve provides better discrimination.

3. Tanh's Balance:
   - Good performance and generalization
   - Possible explanation: Tanh's zero-centered output might lead to more stable gradients during training, allowing for better overall learning.

4. ReLU and LeakyReLU:
   - Lower performance but lower training loss
   - Possible explanation: These functions might be learning faster (lower training loss) but failing to capture complex, non-linear relationships in the data as effectively as sigmoid or tanh.

5. Overfitting with Sigmoid:
   - Higher test scores, lower validation scores
   - Possible explanation: Sigmoid's tendency to saturate might be causing the model to fit noise in the training data, leading to poorer generalization.

6. Generalization of Tanh and ReLU:
   - Better validation scores
   - Possible explanation: These functions might be creating more robust internal representations, leading to better performance on unseen data.

This trend suggests that the nature of the data and the specific classification task play a crucial role in determining which non-linearity is most effective. The bounded nature of sigmoid and tanh seems to be advantageous in this particular scenario.

   d. AutoEncoder performance:

   e. Choice of k and metric:

   f. Potential overfitting:
   - The original model might be overfitting to noise in the data, which could explain its better performance. The dimensionality reduction might be removing some of this noise, leading to worse performance on the test set but potentially better generalization.
